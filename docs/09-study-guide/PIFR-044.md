# PIFR-044

## Objective

MOS Schedule 3 Section 2.2 Unit 2.2.1 10.1.1: State the safety precautions that must be observed when operating aircraft radar equipment on the ground.

## Why it matters

Ground radar precautions are a direct aircraft and apron safety issue: CAO 20.9 section 6 gives the official operating limits for higher-power aircraft radar equipment on the ground.

## Study notes

- Use CAO 20.9 section 6 as the first hard-copy/source lookup point for aircraft radar equipment ground-operation precautions.
- Section 6.2 applies to radar equipment with nominal peak power output above 25 kW.
- With the antenna rotating in normal mode, the scanned sector must be clear to 37 m from the antenna of refuelling/defuelling aircraft, fuel tankers/tanks/storage areas, persons or cargo, and other aircraft or hangars.
- With the antenna stationary and the beam directed toward those objects, separation must exceed 60 m.
- The 37 m and 60 m distances may be reduced by 75% when an approved beam attenuating device is used.
- Do not energise radar in a hangar or enclosure unless a suitable microwave energy absorbing shield is fitted over the antenna.
- Do not operate the equipment in an aircraft that is being refuelled or defuelled.
- Currency note: this is taken from supplied Federal Register compilation F2011C00881, prepared 11 November 2011. Independently verify the latest authorised compilation before treating currency as complete.

## Common CASA traps

- Do not confuse airborne weather radar equipment carriage references with ground-operation safety precautions.
- Do not apply the 37 m normal-mode clearance to a stationary beam; section 6.2.2 uses 60 m.
- Do not forget the threshold: section 6 applies to radar equipment with nominal peak power output above 25 kW.
- Do not operate radar during refuelling or defuelling.

## Exam use

- For the exam index, start with CAO 20.9 section 6 when the question asks about operating aircraft radar equipment on the ground.
- Look for the section heading `Ground operation of aircraft radar equipment`, then read clauses 6.1 and 6.2.
- Memorise the lookup pattern, not just the numbers: applicability above 25 kW, rotating antenna 37 m, stationary beam 60 m, attenuator reduction, hangar shielding, and no operation while refuelling/defuelling.
